Part B: Report

Description of Project:

Biostimulant and remineralisation trial (alternative dataset). An alternative trial comparing a seaweed-extract biostimulant and a rock-dust remineralisation treatment against a baseline practice. Two replicates per treatment were assessed. Direct cost components and yields were recorded per hectare and analysed with declining-rate discounting over an eight-year horizon.

Interpretation of Main Findings:

Under the current analysis settings, the selected treatment is compared against the control. Seaweed extract has an estimated net present value of $8,573.91 per hectare, a benefit-cost ratio of 5.42, a gross profit margin of 81.5%, and a return on investment of 441.9%. Relative to Baseline practice, the estimated difference in net present value is $4,168.05 per hectare. Seaweed extract also records the highest net present value of all treatments analysed.

The ranking table reports all treatments using average yield, average direct costs, present value of benefits, present value of costs, net present value, benefit-cost ratio, gross profit margin, return on investment, and the difference in net present value from the control. Sensitivity Analysis:

The sensitivity analysis tests alternative discount rates, grain prices, benefit assumptions, and cost assumptions for Seaweed extract. The estimated net present value ranges from $7,544.74 per hectare to $13,437.02 per hectare across the scenarios currently entered in the tool.
